1 Data Genesis

The foundation of CharityAI is its strictly curated dataset, comprising 264 high-impact nongovernmental organizations (NGOs). We believe that the integrity of a recommendation engine is directly proportional to the quality of its input. Therefore, our data genesis protocol follows a multi-tier ingestion pipeline designed to filter out ambiguity and focus exclusively on high-performance entities.

Each entry in the `data.js` master file has been primary-sourced from verified global disclosure portals. This includes cross-referencing IRS Form 990 data for US-based organizations, UK Charity Commission filings, and local regulatory certifications across India, Kenya, South Africa, and beyond.

Direct Source Verification Pillar:

Unlike standard scrapers, our engine validates the Unique Identification Number (UIN) for every charity before permanent inclusion. If a charity's registration status is not active or in good standing with its respective national regulator, it is automatically purged from the recommendation pool.

We analyze historical reputation back to 2022, ensuring that the charities presented are not merely one-year wonders but established pillars of the philanthropic community with a sustainable trajectory.

2 The Cortex Engine v4.2

The "Cortex Engine" is our proprietary matching protocol. It moves beyond simple keyword filtering to implement a **multi-vector semantic analysis**. When a user selects a cause—say, "Education & Literacy" in "Kenya"—the engine doesn't just look for those strings; it evaluates the charity against the user's donor persona.

The weighting algorithm is designed to prioritize dollar-for-dollar efficacy. We believe that a donor's most important metric is how much of their contribution reaches the actual beneficiary. Therefore, the engine applies a heavy scalar to the Program Expense Ratio.

/** * Cortex Protocol v4.2 Weighting Matrix * Goal: Maximize realistic impact & mission alignment */ function calculateProtocolScore(donorState, charity) { // 1. Mission Convergence (Max +25 points) let score = semanticMap(donorState.cause, charity.primary_cause); // 2. Geographic Proximity (Max +25 points) if (charity.country === donorState.location) score += 25; else if (charity.operating_regions.includes(donorState.location)) score += 15; // 3. Efficiency Multiplier (Max +10 points) // Weighted bonus for program ratios exceeding 85% const efficiency = charity.program_expense_ratio; if (efficiency > 0.85) score += (efficiency - 0.85) * 66.7; // 4. Trust Vector Scalar (Max +10 points) const trust = charity.trust_score; if (trust > 90) score += (trust - 90); return clamp(score, 10, 99); }

This protocol ensures that small, high-efficiency local organizations have the opportunity to outrank massive global charities that may have higher overheads but lower direct impact ratios.

4 Forensic Financial Modeling

Our "AI Intelligence" tab is powered by what we call Forensic Financial Modeling. We don't just display the revenue; we break it down to see how money moves through the organization. We analyze three critical ratios:

  1. Program Expense Ratio: The lifeblood of the charity. We look for >80% as the "Gold Standard."
  2. Administrative Overhead: We analyze executive compensation and internal logistics costs to ensure they stay within ethical bounds relative to the sector.
  3. Fundraising Efficiency: We measure how much it costs the charity to raise $1. High-efficiency charities keep this below $0.15.

This data is not static. Our `data.js` architecture is designed to be updated per fiscal year, currently reflecting the most audited data from late 2024 and early 2025. When you see a "Transparency Score" of 100, it means that organization has provided full, audited visibility into all three of these ratios.
